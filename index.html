Research Findings on Bioenergy & Sustainable Power

Malaysia's Agricultural Biomass

In 2022, Malaysia generated 182.6 million tonnes of total biomass [cite: 7, 43]. The vast majority comes from plantation biomass, which accounts for 164 million tonnes [cite: 42]. The palm oil industry contributes roughly 94% of this total [cite: 59]. Instead of environmentally harmful disposal methods like open burning, converting this agricultural waste to energy offers a pathway to a circular economy [cite: 4, 5].

Waste-to-Energy Processes

Agricultural biomass is converted into energy through four main technological pathways [cite: 46]:

  • Direct Combustion: Biomass is burned to produce high-pressure steam, which drives a turbine to generate electricity [cite: 47].
  • Anaerobic Digestion: Microorganisms break down organic waste in the absence of oxygen to produce biogas (methane and CO2) [cite: 48].
  • Gasification & Pyrolysis: Thermochemical processes that convert biomass into syngas, bio-oil, and biochar using high temperatures [cite: 49, 50].
  • Torrefaction & Pelletization: Physical and mild heat treatments that increase energy density, turning biomass into wood pellets [cite: 52].

ASEAN Biomass Policies

Across ASEAN, national laws and strategies are evolving to harness the potential of biomass [cite: 56]. Key examples include:

  • Malaysia: Governed by the Renewable Energy Act 2011 and managed by a Feed-in Tariff (FiT) mechanism that allows producers to sell electricity to the national grid [cite: 54, 61]. As of 2020, Malaysia reached approximately 211 MW of installed biomass power capacity [cite: 60].
  • Singapore: Focuses primarily on R&D through the Sustainable Singapore Blueprint, as there is no specific biomass law [cite: 54].

Sembcorp Hydrogen-Ready Plant

A new 600MW hydrogen-ready combined cycle power plant (CCPP) is being developed for Sembcorp in Singapore, expected to be operational by 2026 [cite: 74]. It utilizes the Mitsubishi Power M701JAC gas turbine [cite: 76]. Because it simultaneously produces steam for industrial processes, the plant achieves an efficiency of over 60% [cite: 77, 87].

Hydrogen Blending Implementation

The Sembcorp plant does not run on 100% hydrogen initially [cite: 79]. During Phase 1, it runs primarily on Natural Gas but is capable of burning a blend of 30% Hydrogen by volume with zero hardware modifications [cite: 80]. In Phase 2, the turbine is designed to be retrofitted to burn 100% Hydrogen once the regional supply chain becomes commercially viable [cite: 81]. This is estimated to save about 220,000 tonnes of CO2 per year compared to older technology [cite: 89].

Environmental Impacts of Biomass

Biomass energy has notable environmental implications:

  • Positive: It promotes carbon neutrality, improves air quality by eliminating open-field burning, and supports a circular economy by transforming disposal problems into resources [cite: 65].
  • Risks: Potential loss of biodiversity and habitat if land is cleared for new "energy crops" [cite: 65]. In Malaysia, this is mitigated by MSPO certification to ensure biomass does not come from deforested areas [cite: 66].
